Eons later, there is a little box without a top.

A white hospital room box. Four walls, counterclockwise from north:

   1. the wall with the window,
   2. the wall with the painting of flowers,
   3. the wall with the door and maybe a little table,
     and
   4. the wall with the patient.

Everything else in the entire world is featureless and white.

The patient is awake, and she is hooked up to many important devices. She has my eyes. Her skin is black, the same starlight black as mine. She has my hair, expansive and curly, though it is pulled back by a headband. Her body is beneath a sheet, and may or may not exist.

Beside her sits the mother, who looks just like my mother, and the father, who also looks like mine. They have both been crying. Are currently crying, their faces frozen between joy and grief as tears roll out, like flesh statues in a fountain.

Next to them stands a wrinkled auntie in a white coat. On the other side of the patient, a nurse waits.

But then the box changes all at once, and now the mother is leaning forward and holding the girl, and her shaking vibrates the girl's frame, and the smell of her vibrates the girl's brain, and for a moment I am the girl, in the room with smell and many sounds, where movement and bodies are happening now, and breathing is happening now, and mama is holding me, papa is leaning in to hug us both, the doctor beside them is lowering her tablet.

This moment happens several times.

Each time through I think it will be different, but it isn't. My eyes meet the doctor's, and they are old and wise and they tell me it's okay to stay there. I want to, but the body is rolling with sensations, and smells, and its being moved, and it's all too much, there are too many moments and they just keep happening and I fall off, back to the box.

The little people in the little box are moving now, like they're alive. The mother is holding the patient. The father has his arm around the mother, his hand squeezing the patient's leg.

"Can you hear me, Jules?" the doctor asks in her little, far away voice.

Mama sniffles, and the sound happens again and again to the body. There seem to be too many moments, in the body. They are getting jumbled and layering atop one another impossibly.
